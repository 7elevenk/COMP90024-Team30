A Connected City:

In a connected city, all people and goods can move to, from and within the city efficiently. Catering for growth and safeguarding prosperity will require planning for an efficient and sustainable transport network.

Technology and innovative forms of movement will play a significant role in changing the way people and goods move. The responsible agencies will collaborate with stakeholders to implement measures that make it easier for more people to make sustainable and smart choices as they travel to and around the city, whether by foot, bicycle, tram, bus, train or car.

We can investigate the current state of physical and social connectedness of Melbourne and how one feeds into the other (maybe we can derive some insights on that).
AURIN Data:
  • There is a lot of data on road networks, walking paths other physical structures like arterial roads, bridges etc. In summary, a lot of data on public infrastructure.
  • These datasets can be exploited to formulate a picture of physical connectivity within Melbourne.
  • One of the concerns identified in the research was there are very well-connected hubs within Melbourne but the hubs themselves are not very well connected -> don’t consider Melbourne as a whole but think of neighbourhoods as self-contained places rather than network of places.
Twitter Data:
  • Twitter data can be used to understand the social connectivity of Melbourne.
  • We will need geo-tagged twitter data – something accessible within the context of this assignment.
  • We will not have to rely as much on the newly generated data. The new data can also tell us about the change in the social connectivity.
  • Several papers have explored in using Twitter geotagged data to design network measures of connectedness
